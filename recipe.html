Japanese fried rice is a delicious, quick weeknight dinner made with day-old rice, fresh vegetables, eggs, and savory seasonings. It comes together in under 30 minutes and is endlessly customizable with whatever you have on hand.

Ingredients

For main dish

2 cups cooked Japanese short-grain rice, day-old
3 large eggs, lightly beaten
200g chicken breast, diced into small cubes
1 cup frozen peas and carrots, thawed
3 spring onions, thinly sliced

For the sauce

3 tbsp Japanese soy sauce (shoyu)
1 tbsp sesame oil
1 tsp oyster sauce and pinch of white pepper

Directions

1. Prepare the cold rice and aromatics

Use day-old Japanese short-grain rice that has been refrigerated overnight. Cold rice separates easily and prevents clumping in the wok. Break apart any large clumps with your fingers before you begin. Dice half a white onion, mince 3 garlic cloves, and thinly slice 2 green onion stalks. Crack 2 eggs into a small bowl and beat lightly with a pinch of salt. Set everything beside the stove — once the wok is hot, there is no time to prep.

Lay out your soy sauce, sesame oil, and white pepper within arm's reach before turning on the heat. Yakimeshi moves fast and every second counts once the wok starts smoking. Having a clean, organized station is just as important as the ingredients themselves for achieving that authentic restaurant-quality result at home.

2. Sear on the highest heat possible

Heat a wok or cast-iron skillet over the strongest flame your stove allows. Add 2 tablespoons of neutral oil and wait until it just begins to smoke. Add the garlic and onion, stir-frying for 60 seconds until fragrant and lightly golden. Push the aromatics aside, pour in the beaten eggs, scramble quickly, then fold everything together before the eggs fully set. Add the cold rice and press it flat against the hot wok surface for 30 to 45 seconds without touching it — this develops the signature smoky wok hei flavor that defines great yakimeshi.

3. Season, finish, and plate immediately

Drizzle 1½ tablespoons of soy sauce and ½ teaspoon of toasted sesame oil around the edges of the wok so they hit the hot metal and caramelize before coating the rice. Season generously with white pepper — this is the secret spice of yakimeshi. Toss everything over high heat for one final minute. Serve in a mound or press into a bowl and invert for a restaurant-style dome. Garnish with sliced green onions and toasted sesame seeds, and serve immediately while steaming hot.
